üéì About Me

I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the Department of Statistics at the University of Michigan, working with Prof. Ji Zhu and Prof. Gongjun Xu. I received my Ph.D. in Statistics from Beijing Normal University (BNU), where I was fortunate to be advised by Prof. Lixing Zhu.

My research revolves around developing and applying novel statistical methodologies to tackle challenges posed by complex data. I am particularly passionate about:

  • Statistical Inference for complex data structures
  • Model-Structure Detection and specification testing
  • High-Dimensional Data analysis and change point detection
  • Machine Learning methods with statistical guarantees
